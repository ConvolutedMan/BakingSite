Tuxedo Brownies (for when a casual brownie won't do)

I have a love/hate relationship with these brownies. On one hand they are delicious and are generally crowd pleasers. On the other hand these are a pain in the ass to make.


You'll notice that the first ingredient is a pack of brownie mix, that's because this recipe is more about the filling than the brownie. If you want a brownie recipe you can find one here.

Quick facts

Difficulty: Difficult

Prep time: 40 minutes

Bake time 30 minutes

Total time: 70 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 package of brownie mis (plus ingredients to make cake-like brownies*)
  • 2 oz white chocolate
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 Cup powdered sugar
  • 1 Cup thawed whipped topping**

instructions

Brownies

  1. Make brownies according to instructions, making sure to make them cake-like
  2. Place brownie batter into mini muffin pan, filling each cup about halfway
  3. Bake for 14 minutes. DON'T OVERBAKE
  4. Remove pan to cooling rack, and cool for 15 minutes
  5. Immediately after placing brownies on cooling rack, indent each brownie
  6. Remove brownies from pan

Filling

  1. Combine chocolate and milk
  2. Microwave 1 minute on high, stir until smooth
  3. Cool slightly
  4. Combine cream cheese and powdered sugar
  5. Add to chocolate
  6. Fold in whipped topping
  7. Put cream cheese mixture into cooled brownie cups
  8. Keep in airtight container
  9. Refrigerate

helpful hints and tricks

I cannot stress how important using a flour spray on the muffin pan is! It makes getting the brownies out of the pan significantly easier

When filling the pan with batter, I used to use a cookie scoop filled about halfway. However, lately I've taken to using a pancake batter dropper.

I use a plastic measuring spoon to indent the brownies. I find using metal causes the brownies to stick to the metal and tear them.

Speaking of getting the brownies out of the pan, I will generally try to twist them out, but if that doesn't work try loosening it with a butter knife. There is a strong chance you'll tear a few brownies at this point. I still do all the time.

When filling the brownie cups with filling, I use a piping bag, but you can use whatever you want.

*Genrally speaking adding a single egg to brownies will make them cake like.

**I actually like to make my own whipped cream. Take 1 Cup heavy whipping cream and beat it until stiff peaks form. Add about 1/8 Cup sugar and 1 capful of vanilla. Add more sugar or vanilla if you are unsatisfied with the taste.
